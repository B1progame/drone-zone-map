# Why Aeris exists

![Aeris live context](../public/screenshots/home.jpg)

I built Aeris because flight planning often forces a pilot to jump between a map, weather service, authority website, and personal notes. The project brings those pieces together while keeping the important boundary visible: a readable planning tool is not legal permission to fly.

The design goals are:

- privacy by default, with saved places and routes kept locally;
- official-source links and source limitations kept visible;
- calm progressive detail instead of an unreadable dashboard;
- offline map support for unreliable connectivity;
- honest failure states rather than invented coverage.

The project is maintained by [B1progame](https://github.com/B1progame). Contributions and issue reports should preserve provider attribution, licensing boundaries, and the distinction between situational awareness and authorization.
